Checklists

Quick, practical checklists to help you stay organized and prepared throughout the first 45 days.

Checklist: Days 0–7

  • Contact a lawyer or duty counsel
  • Request disclosure
  • Start documenting everything
  • Attend all meetings
  • Keep communication calm and written

Checklist: Days 7–21

  • Review disclosure with your lawyer
  • Begin recommended services
  • Attend all access visits
  • Document all interactions
  • Prepare for the case conference

Checklist: Days 21–45

  • Continue all services consistently
  • Strengthen documentation
  • Prepare for court with your lawyer
  • Maintain strong access visits
  • Avoid common mistakes

Checklist: Access Visits

  • Arrive early
  • Bring activities
  • Stay child‑focused
  • Document each visit
  • Avoid discussing the case

Checklist: Communication With CAS

  • Use email whenever possible
  • Keep messages brief and respectful
  • Stick to facts, not emotions
  • Save all communication
  • Follow through on commitments

Court Day Checklist

  • Arrive 20–30 minutes early
  • Bring all documents and notes
  • Bring a notebook and pen
  • Dress clean and neutral
  • Turn off your phone or silence it
  • Stay calm and respectful at all times
  • Speak to duty counsel if you need help

Access Visit Checklist

  • Arrive early
  • Bring snacks or drinks (if allowed)
  • Bring activities (books, colouring, games)
  • Bring diapers, wipes, or supplies for young children
  • Stay positive and child‑focused
  • Do not discuss the case with your child
  • Document the visit afterward

Service Engagement Checklist

  • Register for recommended programs quickly
  • Keep proof of attendance
  • Save emails, confirmations, and certificates
  • Attend consistently — no missed sessions
  • Update your lawyer on your progress
  • Ask for written summaries from service providers
