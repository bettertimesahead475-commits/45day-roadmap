Legal Support

  • Legal Aid Ontario – Financial help for eligible parents
  • Duty Counsel – Free legal assistance at the courthouse
  • Family Law Information Centres (FLIC) – Information and guidance
  • Law Society Referral Service – Free 30‑minute consultation with a lawyer

Legal support is essential — do not navigate the process alone if you can avoid it.

Parenting & Family Support

  • Parenting programs (local community centres, family resource centres)
  • EarlyON Child & Family Centres
  • Family counselling services
  • Domestic violence support programs (if relevant)

Engaging in parenting and family programs shows commitment and progress.

Mental Health & Counselling

  • Community mental health agencies
  • Walk‑in counselling clinics
  • Private therapists (sliding scale options available)
  • Support groups for stress, trauma, or parenting challenges

Taking care of your mental health helps you stay grounded and focused.

Addictions Support (If Relevant)

  • Local addictions treatment centres
  • Outpatient counselling programs
  • 12‑step programs (AA, NA)
  • Harm reduction services

CAS and the court look for consistent engagement and effort.

Community Support

  • Indigenous support organizations
  • Settlement services (for newcomers)
  • Housing support programs
