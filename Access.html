Access (Visits)

How access works, what CAS and the court look for, and how to make every visit count.

Why Access Matters

Access visits are one of the strongest indicators of your relationship with your child. CAS and the court pay close attention to:

  • Your consistency
  • Your punctuality
  • Your preparation
  • Your interactions with your child
  • Your ability to stay calm and child‑focused

Strong access visits help support reunification.

1. Types of Access

Access can be:

  • Supervised — monitored by CAS or a third party
  • Semi‑supervised — some monitoring, some independence
  • Unsupervised — full independence
  • Virtual — video or phone calls

The level of access can change as you show progress.

2. How to Prepare for Visits

Being prepared shows responsibility and care. Bring:

  • Snacks or drinks (if allowed)
  • Activities (colouring, games, books)
  • Comfort items (if your child has favourites)
  • Diapers, wipes, or supplies for younger children

Arrive early — even 10 minutes early makes a strong impression.

3. What CAS Looks For During Visits

Workers observe:

  • Your ability to stay calm and patient
  • Your focus on your child’s needs
  • Your ability to redirect behaviour positively
  • Your emotional connection with your child
  • Your ability to follow rules and boundaries

They are not looking for perfection — they are looking for effort, safety, and connection.

4. What NOT to Do During Visits

  • Do not discuss the case with your child
  • Do not speak negatively about CAS or caregivers
  • Do not argue with workers
  • Do not use your phone unless necessary
  • Do not bring other adults unless approved

Visits should be positive, safe, and child‑focused.
