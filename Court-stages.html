Court Stages Explained

A clear breakdown of each stage in a CYFSA child protection case — what it is, what happens, and what parents should expect.

1. First Appearance

This is the first time your case appears in court. It is usually brief. The purpose is to:

  • Confirm you understand the concerns
  • Confirm whether you have a lawyer
  • Request disclosure
  • Set the next court date

No major decisions are usually made here.

2. Case Conference

A judge meets with you, your lawyer, CAS, and their lawyer to discuss the issues. The goal is to:

  • Identify the main concerns
  • Discuss possible solutions
  • Review your progress
  • See if any agreements can be reached

Judges often give guidance here. This is a key opportunity to show cooperation and effort.

3. Motions

A motion is a request for the court to make a temporary decision. Motions may deal with:

  • Access (visits)
  • Assessments
  • Safety concerns
  • Service requirements

Your lawyer will help you prepare if a motion is needed.

4. Settlement Discussions

Many cases resolve through agreements rather than trial. Settlement discussions may include:

  • Updated access schedules
  • Service plan adjustments
  • Timelines for reunification
  • Expectations for both you and CAS

Reaching agreements can reduce conflict and move the case forward faster.

5. Trial

If no agreement is reached, the case may go to trial. At trial:

  • CAS presents evidence
  • You and your lawyer present your evidence
  • Witnesses may testify
  • The judge makes final decisions

Most cases do not reach trial, but it is important to understand the possibility.

Important

At every stage, your consistency, communication, and progress matter. Judges look for patterns — showing effort early and often makes a real difference.
