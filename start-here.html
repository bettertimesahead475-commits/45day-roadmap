Start Here

A clear, calm introduction to CYFSA court and what to expect in the first days.

What CYFSA Court Is

CYFSA court handles child protection cases in Ontario. When the Children’s Aid Society (CAS) becomes involved, the court oversees decisions about safety, services, access, and long‑term planning. The process can feel overwhelming — this guide breaks it down into clear steps.

Your First Priorities

  • Read every document CAS gives you.
  • Get a lawyer — duty counsel, Legal Aid, or private.
  • Show up to every court date without exception.
  • Stay organized — keep a folder or binder for all paperwork.
  • Stay calm and respectful in all communication.

Documents You Will Receive

You will usually receive several key documents early in the process:

  • Protection Application — explains why CAS is involved.
  • Plan of Care — what CAS wants to happen with your child.
  • Affidavits — sworn statements from CAS or others.
  • Disclosure — evidence CAS is relying on.

These documents can be emotional to read. Take breaks if needed, but make sure you understand them. Your lawyer can help explain anything unclear.

Your Rights

  • The right to know the concerns.
  • The right to respond to allegations.
  • The right to have a lawyer.
  • The right to receive disclosure (evidence).
  • The right to participate in planning.
  • The right to request access (visits).

What Happens Next

Most cases follow a similar early timeline:

  • First Appearance — you confirm you understand the case and whether you have a lawyer.
  • Disclosure — CAS provides the evidence they are relying on.
  • Case Conference — a judge helps everyone discuss the issues and possible solutions.

These early steps set the tone for the rest of the case. Showing cooperation, organization, and progress makes a strong impression.

Important

Do not discuss the case on social media. Do not miss court dates. Do not ignore CAS requests. These are the most common mistakes that hurt parents early in the process.
