Days 0–7

Your first week in CYFSA court: what happens, what to expect, and what to do immediately.

Your First 7 Days Matter

The first week sets the foundation for your entire case. Judges and CAS pay close attention to how you respond early on — your organization, your communication, and your willingness to engage in services all make a strong impression.

1. The First Appearance

This is usually your first court date. It is not a trial and no major decisions are made here. The purpose is to:

  • Confirm you understand why CAS is involved.
  • Tell the court whether you have a lawyer.
  • Request disclosure (the evidence CAS is relying on).
  • Set the next court date.

Be respectful, calm, and clear. Judges notice your tone and attitude.

2. Get Disclosure

Disclosure is the evidence CAS is using in your case. It may include:

  • Worker notes
  • Incident reports
  • School or medical information
  • Affidavits
  • Police reports (if applicable)

Read it carefully. Highlight anything that is incorrect or missing. Your lawyer can help you respond to inaccuracies.

3. Meet or Contact Your Lawyer

Whether you have duty counsel, Legal Aid, or a private lawyer, make sure you:

  • Share all documents you received.
  • Ask them to explain anything unclear.
  • Discuss your goals (access, reunification, services).
  • Ask what you should start doing immediately.

4. Start Services Immediately
