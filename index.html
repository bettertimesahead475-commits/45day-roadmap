🚨 Phase 1
Days 1–5: Emergency Response
The first five days are the most consequential of your entire case. Every action or inaction here echoes forward. Move fast, stay calm, document everything.
⚠️
If your child has been removed: CAS must bring you to court within 5 days. This is the law — CYFSA s.75. Do not wait for them to call you. Call Legal Aid Ontario immediately: 1-800-668-8258

Script: First CAS phone call

Use these words
"I want to cooperate with your concerns. Before we go any further, I'm going to need everything in writing. I am also going to be consulting with a lawyer before I agree to anything or make any statements. Can you confirm your name, your worker number, and your direct phone number? I am writing this down."
Know your Charter rights: You have the right to be free from unreasonable search and seizure (s.8), the right not to be arbitrarily detained (s.9), and the right to life, liberty and security of the person (s.7). CAS cannot enter your home without consent or a court order — unless they believe a child is in immediate danger.
📞 Phase 2
Days 6–15: Investigation Management
CAS is gathering information. This is where many parents inadvertently harm their own case. Every word, every gesture, every missed call is being noted.
Script: Responding to a home visit request
"Yes, I'm available on [date]. I'll have a support person present. For my records, can you confirm in writing what the purpose of this visit is and what you'll be looking at? I want to make sure I have everything ready for you."
📄 Phase 3
Days 16–25: Evidence & Documentation
Build your evidentiary foundation. Courts decide based on evidence, not emotion. Everything you gather now becomes part of your defense.
⚖️ Phase 4
Days 26–35: Legal Strategy & Court Prep
If this is heading to court, you need to understand the process and be prepared. Even with a lawyer, the more you know, the better positioned you are.
Key legal standard: Under CYFSA s.74(2), CAS must establish that the child is or is likely to be in need of protection on a balance of probabilities. You do not have to prove you're a perfect parent — you need to establish that the risk alleged doesn't meet the legal threshold.
🎯 Phase 5
Days 36–45: Strategic Positioning
By now you've built your foundation. These final days before the critical hearing window are about presenting the strongest version of yourself and your parenting.
Parenting capacity statement — structure to follow
"I understand the concerns that were raised about [specific concern]. Since [date], I have taken the following steps: [list specific actions]. I have completed [programs]. I have maintained [stability factors]. I understand my responsibilities as a parent and I'm committed to continued growth. My support system includes [people]. My plan going forward is [concrete plan]."
📌 Ongoing
Long-Term Defense Foundation
The 45 days are a sprint. What you build after them is the marathon. CAS cases rarely end at Day 45 — protect yourself for the long run.
