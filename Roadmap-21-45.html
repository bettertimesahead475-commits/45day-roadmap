Days 21–45

The final phase of the early CYFSA timeline: motions, negotiations, documentation, and preparing for long‑term planning.

Why Days 21–45 Matter

By this stage, CAS and the court are looking for patterns — consistency, effort, communication, and progress. What you do in this period can influence access, timelines, and the overall direction of your case.

1. Motions (If They Happen)

A motion is a request for the court to make a temporary decision. Common motions include:

  • Changes to access (more time, supervised to unsupervised, etc.)
  • Requests for assessments
  • Requests for specific services
  • Concerns about safety or compliance

Your lawyer will guide you through whether a motion is necessary or how to respond to one.

2. Negotiations and Agreements

Many cases move toward agreements during this period. These may include:

  • Updated access schedules
  • Service plan adjustments
  • Timelines for next steps
  • Expectations for both you and CAS

Agreements can help avoid unnecessary conflict and keep the case moving forward.

3. Strengthening Your Documentation

Documentation becomes extremely important during this phase. Keep organized records of:

  • Access visits (dates, times, activities, positives)
  • Service attendance (proof, confirmations, certificates)
  • Communication with CAS (emails, messages)
  • Any progress you’ve made

Good documentation helps your lawyer advocate for you and shows the court your commitment.

4. Reviewing Your Progress

CAS and the court will look at whether you have:

  • Engaged in services consistently
  • Attended all access visits
  • Communicated respectfully
  • Followed recommendations
  • Shown stability and effort

Even small steps matter — progress is progress.

5. Preparing for the Next Court Date

By the end of the first 45 days, you should be prepared for the next major court appearance. This may include:

  • Updated disclosure
  • Service updates
  • Access reports
  • CAS recommendations
  • Your lawyer’s plan moving forward

Being organized and prepared helps your lawyer advocate more effectively.

Important

Do not stop services. Do not miss visits. Do not send emotional messages to CAS. Consistency during this period is one of the strongest indicators of long‑term success in your case.
