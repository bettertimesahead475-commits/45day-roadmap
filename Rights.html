Your Rights

A clear, simple explanation of your rights as a parent involved in a CYFSA child protection case.

Why Knowing Your Rights Matters

Parents often feel powerless when CAS becomes involved. Understanding your rights helps you stay informed, make better decisions, and advocate for yourself throughout the process.

1. The Right to Know the Concerns

CAS must tell you why they are involved and what their concerns are. You have the right to ask for clarification if anything is unclear.

2. The Right to Disclosure

You have the right to receive the evidence CAS is relying on. This includes:

  • Worker notes
  • Affidavits
  • Reports
  • Incident summaries

Your lawyer can help you review and respond to disclosure.

3. The Right to a Lawyer

You have the right to legal representation. Options include:

  • Legal Aid Ontario
  • Duty counsel at the courthouse
  • Private counsel

Do not navigate the process alone if you can avoid it.

4. The Right to Participate in Planning

You have the right to be involved in decisions about your child’s care, services, and long‑term planning. CAS must include you in discussions and planning meetings.

5. The Right to Respond to Allegations

You have the right to give your side of the story. This may happen through:

  • Affidavits
  • Conversations with your lawyer
  • Case conferences
  • Court hearings

Your voice matters — use it calmly and clearly.

6. The Right to Request Access

If your child is not in your care, you have the right to request access (visits). The court decides access based on the child’s best interests, but you have the right to ask.

7. The Right to Appeal Certain Decisions

Some decisions can be appealed. Your lawyer can explain which decisions qualify and whether an appeal is appropriate.

Important

Your rights do not disappear because CAS is involved. Staying informed and organized helps you protect your rights and move your case forward.
