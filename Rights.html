Your Rights

A clear summary of your legal rights under the CYFSA during the first 45 days of a child protection case.

1. Your Right to a Lawyer

You have the right to legal representation. If you cannot afford a lawyer, you may qualify for Legal Aid or can speak with duty counsel.

2. Your Right to Disclosure

You have the right to see the evidence CAS is using in your case. This includes notes, reports, allegations, and documentation.

3. Your Right to Participate in Court

You have the right to attend all court appearances, speak with your lawyer, and understand the case being made against you.

4. Your Right to Be Treated Fairly

CAS must act in good faith, follow the law, and treat you with respect. You have the right to ask questions and request clarification.

5. Your Right to Access (Unless Restricted)

In most cases, you have the right to see your child unless a court orders otherwise. Access should be meaningful and consistent.

6. Your Right to Appeal or Challenge Decisions

You can challenge CAS decisions through your lawyer, through court motions, or through internal complaint processes.
